TA'ing @ Brown + MIT

Current Courses

  • MIT, Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering
    • 1.200 Transportation Systems Analysis: Performance and Optimization (Fall 2015)
    • Teaching Assistant. Lecturers: Carolina Osorio Pizano, James Orlin
    • Syllabus | Course Website
    • Recitation Notes
    • TA Evaluation: (Not available yet)
    • Problem-motivated introduction to methods, models and tools for the analysis and design of transportation networks including their planning, operations and control. Topics include: capacity of critical elements of transportation networks; traffic flows and deterministic and probabilistic delay models; formulation of optimization models for planning and scheduling of freight, transit and airline systems, and their solution using software packages; user- and system-optimal traffic assignment; control of traffic flows on highways, urban grids, and airspace; stochastic simulation modeling of traffic flows.

    • 15.730 Data, Models, and Decisions (Executive MBA Core) (Spring 2015)
    • Teaching Assistant. Lecturer: Georgia Perakis
    • Syllabus | Course Website
    • Recitation Notes
    • The course introduces students to the basic tools for using data to build models in order to make informed management decisions. Covers introductory probability, decision analysis, basic statistics, regression, simulation, linear and nonlinear optimization, and discrete optimization. Computer spreadsheet exercises, cases, and examples drawn from marketing, finance, operations management, and other management functions.

    • ENGN0900 Managerial Decision Making (Spring 2012)
    • Undergraduate Teaching Assistant. Lecturer: Barrett Hazeltine
    • Ways of making effective decisions in managerial situations, especially situations with a significant technological component; decision analysis; time value of money; competitive situations; forecasting; planning and scheduling; manufacturing strategy; corporate culture.   • Brown, Department of Computer Science
    • CSCI0040 Introduction to Scientific Computing and Problem Solving (Spring 2012)
    • Undergraduate Teaching Assistant. Lecturer: Maurice P. Herlihy
    • An introduction to computer programming and software design in a high-level language. Emphasizes fundamental techniques and strategies for solving scientific problems with computers. Illustrates abstract concepts with a wide range of exemplary applications from engineering, the sciences, and the humanities. Intended for students who want a single application-oriented programming course. This course is not intended for computer science concentrators.
