Meet Park

Short Bio

I'm a third-year graduate student at Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) pursuing a Sc.M. in Computation for Design and Optmization (CDO). Advised by Professor Georgia Perakis and Professor Yanchong Karen Zheng from the Operations Research Center and MIT Sloan School of Management, I am working on deepening the analysis of consumer behaviors in a pricing and optimization context. My current research interests include data-driven and quantitative approaches to behavioral operations management. I graduated from Brown University with a Sc.B. in Computer Engineering and Applied Mathematics-Economics in May 2012. I previously worked at Goldman Sachs as a Securities Strats Summer Analyst in 2014, and spent a year at Oracle as a Software Engineer (Fusion Financials Technology) from 2012 to 2013.

Growing up in Bangkok, Thailand, I attended Kasetsart University Laboratory School (OKUS32) and Triam Udom Suksa School (TU67 WitCom14 667#11). My other interests include food, traveling, board games, graphic design, karaoke, and photography. Starting in February 2016, I will be working as a Data Scientist/Consultant in Strategies and Operations at Deloitte Consulting.
