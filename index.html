Park Sinchaisri is currently a PhD Student in Operations Research at Wharton. He received a Sc.B. in Computer Engineering and Applied Mathematics-Economics from Brown University in 2012 and a S.M. in Optimization from MIT in 2016, advised by Georgia Perakis and Yanchong Karen Zheng. His masters thesis "Pricing with Quality Perception: Theory and Experiment" deepens the analysis of consumer behaviors in a pricing and optimization context. Current research interests: behavioral operations, pricing, optimization in transportation systems, public policy, and education.

While at MIT, he served as a President of Thai Students @ MIT, Publicity Officer for Ashdown House, and Social Chair for MIT Association of Computational Science and Engineering Students. Currently at Penn, he is actively involved with the Intercultural Leadership Program, Graduate-Undergraduate Mentoring Program, OLR Student Advisory Board, and TEDxPenn. Professionally, Park had worked for the Institute for Pure and Applied Mathematics at UCLA, American International Assurance, Oracle Corporation, Goldman Sachs, and Deloitte Consulting. [ CV ]
